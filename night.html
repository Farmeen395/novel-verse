Dracula

Bram Stoker’s Dracula is the definitive gothic horror novel that gave birth to the modern image of the vampire. A chilling tale of bloodlust, ancient evil, and psychological terror, the novel unfolds through haunting journal entries and letters. From eerie castles in Transylvania to shadowy streets in Victorian England, every page will leave you breathless and haunted. The novel explores fear of the unknown, Victorian anxieties, and the blurred lines between science and superstition. Stoker masterfully weaves suspense with romantic tragedy, capturing the reader in an atmosphere thick with dread. Count Dracula is not just a monster, but a metaphor for invasion, desire, and decay. As night falls, the boundaries between reality and nightmare vanish, and the characters must confront not only an immortal predator but their deepest fears. Dracula is a timeless masterpiece that continues to influence horror literature, film, and folklore to this day.
