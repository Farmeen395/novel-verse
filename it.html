IT

by Stephen King

"IT" is a terrifying horror novel by Stephen King, first published in 1986. Set in the town of Derry, Maine, the story follows a group of childhood friends who are haunted by a shape-shifting entity that most often appears as a sinister clown named Pennywise. As the creature awakens every 27 years to feed on fear and prey on children, the friends must reunite as adults to face the evil once again. Blending psychological horror, supernatural dread, and coming-of-age themes, "IT" explores trauma, friendship, and the enduring power of memory. It remains one of King's most iconic and unsettling works.
